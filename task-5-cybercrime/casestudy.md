# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing
cybercrimes in India, targeting users through deceptive payment requests
and social engineering tactics. A common scenario involves a college
student, Aarav, who tries to sell his old headphones online. A "buyer"
contacts him and claims to transfer money via UPI. Instead of sending
money, the fraudster sends a "Collect Request" (Request Money) and
instructs Aarav to "accept it to receive payment." Confused but
trusting, Aarav enters his UPI PIN---unintentionally authorizing a debit
from his account.

Step-by-step, the fraud works as follows: 1. The attacker establishes
trust (e.g., posing as a buyer or service agent). 2. They send a collect
request instead of a payment. 3. They use urgency or confusion to get
the victim to enter their UPI PIN. 4. The victim authorizes the
transaction, leading to instant money transfer to the attacker.

Targets typically include students, first-time digital payment users,
and individuals unfamiliar with UPI workflows. The consequences include
immediate financial loss, difficulty in recovery due to rapid fund
transfers, and psychological stress or loss of trust in digital systems.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of understanding that
entering a UPI PIN always means authorizing a payment (debit), not
receiving money.
